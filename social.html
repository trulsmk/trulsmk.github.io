Social Engineering

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. It is one of the most effective methods cybercriminals use because it targets human emotions like fear, urgency, or curiosity rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Common Social Engineering Tactics

Impersonation

Attackers pretend to be someone trusted, such as IT staff, CEOs, or vendors, to gain sensitive access or information.

Pretexting

Creating a fabricated scenario (e.g., claiming there's a system issue) to trick victims into revealing credentials or confidential data.

Baiting

Using a tempting item (like a USB drive labeled “Salary Data”) to trick users into introducing malware into the network.

Tailgating

Physically following an employee into a secure area without proper authentication (e.g., holding the door open).

Quizzes & Fake Surveys

Fun quizzes or online surveys used to collect personal or company-related information from unsuspecting users.

Why This Matters

Social engineering attacks are hard to detect with technology alone. They exploit trust, habit, and social norms. SMEs are often targeted due to smaller teams and less formal procedures. Educating employees is key to prevention.
